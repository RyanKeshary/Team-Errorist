Emergency Protocols

Crucial life-saving procedures for immediate crises. Every second counts. Stay calm, act fast, and prioritize human life.

Verify Safety

Ensure the scene is safe before assisting victims.

Call for Help

Contact emergency services immediately via SOS button.

Protect Vitals

Stop bleeding and maintain clear airways.

Medical Crisis

Immediate Response

Severe Trauma & Bleeding

Apply firm, direct pressure. Do not remove saturated bandages; stack new ones on top. Use a tourniquet if trained.

Unconsciousness (CPR)

Perform 100-120 chest compressions per minute. Push hard and fast in the center of the chest. Ensure airway is clear.

Fire & Smoke

Building Evacuation

Evacuation Path

Stay low to avoid smoke. Feel doors for heat before opening. NEVER use elevators. Meet at pre-designated safe zones.

Stop, Drop, Roll

If clothes catch fire, do not run. Stop immediately, drop to the ground, and roll to smother the flames.

Natural Calamity

Survival Strategy

Earthquake

Drop to hands/knees. Cover head/neck under sturdy furniture. Hold on until shaking stops completely. Stay away from glass.

Flood Action

Seek higher ground immediately. Do not cross moving water by foot or car. Turn off utilities at the main switch.

Hazmat Alert

Biological/Chemical

Shelter-in-Place

Close all windows/doors. Seal openings with tape/plastic. Shut off ventilation systems. Wait for official clearance.

Decontamination

If exposed, remove outer contamination (clothing) and flush involved skin and eyes with large amounts of water.

Emergency "Go-Bag"

Tick off items as you pack them. Saved automatically.

  • Water (3L per person per day)
  • Non-perishable food (3-day supply)
  • First aid kit and medications
  • Flashlight & extra batteries
  • Copies of vital documents

First Aid Basics

Stop Bleeding

Firm, direct pressure for at least 10 minutes without stopping.

Treat Shock

Keep victim warm, elevate feet 12 inches if no head/leg injuries.
